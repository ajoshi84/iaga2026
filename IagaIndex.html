📐 How is the Handicap Index calculated? ▾
1
Score Differential
After each tournament round, a Score Differential is calculated for every player. It adjusts your gross score for the difficulty of the course using Slope Rating and Course Rating — so scores from different courses can be compared fairly.
Diff = (Gross Score − Course Rating) × 113 ÷ Slope Rating
2
Best 2 Differentials
From all your tournament rounds, we take the 2 lowest (best) Score Differentials. This rewards peak performance — your handicap reflects your potential, not your average.
Select the 2 lowest differentials from all rounds played
3
Tournament Index
The two best differentials are averaged, then multiplied by 0.96 — a standard USGA adjustment that accounts for the fact that even low scores may not fully represent your best possible play.
Tournament Index = avg(best 2 diffs) × 0.96
4
NCGA Handicap
Your official NCGA / GHIN Handicap Index reflects your performance across all your rounds at all courses — not just IAGA tournaments. This gives a broader picture of your overall ability.
Sourced from your GHIN member record (NCGA)
5
Usable Handicap
Your Usable Handicap is simply whichever is lower — your Tournament Index or your NCGA Handicap. This ensures the most competitive (lowest) handicap is always used for tournament play.
Usable Handicap = min(Tournament Index, NCGA Handicap)
💡 A minimum of 2 tournament rounds is required before a Tournament Index is calculated. Players with fewer than 2 rounds will not appear in the standings.
📋 Each flight plays from different tee boxes with different Slope and Course Ratings — so differentials are always calculated using the correct tee settings for your flight.
